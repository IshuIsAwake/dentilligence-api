When the model can misfire

The detector was trained on close-up photos of the oral cavity. It can occasionally flag other things that are visually similar to a lesion. Common false-positive sources:

  • Teeth — bright reflections, gaps, or dark crevices can look like patches.
  • Fingers or lips intruding into the frame.
  • Dental equipment — mirrors, retractors, cotton rolls, suction tips.
  • Food debris, lipstick, dyes on the lips or tongue.
  • Glare from camera flash directly on wet tissue.

For best results: open wide, get even light, fill the frame with the inside of your mouth, and crop the photo to the soft tissue area before uploading if teeth or equipment dominate the frame.
